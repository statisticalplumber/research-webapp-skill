Overview

📐

Lie-Admissible Algebras

Nonassociative algebras where the commutator bracket satisfies the Jacobi identity, serving as precursors to Lie algebras.

🔄

Pre-Lie Structures

Algebras with bilinear products where the commutator defines a Lie algebra. Closely related to affine structures on manifolds.

🔗

Geometric Connection

These structures correspond to rich geometric objects on manifolds, extending flat torsion-free affine connections.

Anti-Flexible Algebras (AFAs)

Definition: An AFA is an algebra (𝒜, ⋅) where the associator satisfies:

(x, y, z) = (z, y, x) for all x, y, z ∈ 𝒜

This condition makes AFAs their own opposite algebras and geometrically richer than LSAs or RSAs.

[(x, y, z) = (x⋅y)⋅z − x⋅(y⋅z)]

Key Properties:

  • ✅ Self-opposite algebra (swapping products preserves the structure)
  • ✅ Always Lie-admissible (commutator satisfies Jacobi identity)
  • ✅ Neither commutative nor anticommutative
  • ✅ Semisimple Lie algebras CAN admit AFAs (counterexample: 𝔰𝔩(2,ℂ))

Grading: 𝔤 = 𝔤₋₁ ⊕ 𝔤₀ ⊕ 𝔤₁

Generators: H, E, F

Product (partial):

  • E⋅F = H - 2F
  • F⋅H = 2F
  • H⋅E = 2E

Associated Lie Bracket:

  • [H, E] = 2E, [H, F] = -2F, [E, F] = H
Ex 4.1 A₃-Associative (Quandle)

Product:

  • e₁⋅e₁ = e₁, e₂⋅e₂ = e₂, e₃⋅e₃ = e₃
  • e₁⋅e₂ = e₃, e₂⋅e₃ = e₁, e₃⋅e₁ = e₂
  • e₂⋅e₁ = e₃, e₃⋅e₂ = e₁, e₁⋅e₃ = e₂

Properties:

  • Commutative and nonassociative
  • Diagonal dihedral quandle of order 3

Key Theorems

Theorem 5.1

S₃-associative algebras realize universal pre-Lie structures for all Lie algebras over ℂ.

Proof: Given a Lie algebra, any pre-algebraic structure satisfying the definition has an associator where the signed sum over S₃ equals the Jacobinator J(x₁,x₂,x₃). For Lie algebras, J = 0, which satisfies the S₃-associative condition.

Proposition 2.2

Let (𝒜, ⋅) be an algebra. If 𝒜 satisfies any two of: LSA, RSA, or AFA, then it also satisfies the third.

Proof: Follows from the Akivis identity. If the identity holds for two transpositions τ₁, τ₂, then the remaining conditions follow from the vanishing of the sum.

Corollary

Semisimple Lie algebras over ℂ admit multiple pre-Lie structures: associative algebras, AFAs, A₃-associative, and S₃-associative algebras. The only ones excluded are LSAs and RSAs (for n ≥ 3).

Mathematical Definitions

The Associator

(x, y, z) = (x⋅y)⋅z − x⋅(y⋅z)

The associator measures the failure of associativity. For Lie-admissible algebras, this is generally non-vanishing but satisfies specific symmetry conditions.

The Akivis Identity

[x₁,[x₂,x₃]] + [x₂,[x₃,x₁]] + [x₃,[x₁,x₂]] = ∑_{σ∈S₃} sgn(σ)(xσ(1),xσ(2),xσ(3))

This identity connects the Jacobi identity to the associator. The vanishing of the right-hand side for Lie algebras is equivalent to this being a Lie algebra.

The Commutator

[x, y] = x⋅y − y⋅x

For a pre-Lie structure to be Lie-admissible, this commutator must define a Lie algebra structure (i.e., satisfy the Jacobi identity).
